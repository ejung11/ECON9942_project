Title: Common-Pool Resources and Partial Output-Sharing

Principal Investigator: Professor James C. Cox, Georgia State University

Co-Investigator: Eye Eoun (Ian) Jung, Georgia State University

Sponsor: AYS Dissertation Fellowship

Introduction and Key Information

We invite you to take part in a research study. You will decide if you would like to take part in the study. Your participation in the study will last no more than two hours on one day.

We will ask you to: allocate efforts between two activities. If you do not wish to take part in this study, you can terminate your participation at any time.

Purpose

The purpose of this study is to understand how individuals make decisions in common-pool resource environments and to assess the impact of partial output-sharing arrangements on resource use.

You are invited to participate because you have registered on the Experimental Economics Centerâ€™s website as someone interested in participating in experiments.

We will invite up to 200 people to participate in this study.

Procedures

If you decide to take part in this study, you will:

  • You will allocate efforts between two activities throughout multiple rounds:
    • Activity A (Fixed Return): Each effort invested yields a fixed amount.
    • Activity B (Variable Return): Each effort invested yields a return that depends on the total efforts invested by all participants in your group.
  • Your money payoff will be determined by your responses and the responses of anonymous members in your group.
  • Interact anonymously with other participants; you will not know their identities, and they will not know yours.
  • Fill out a questionnaire.
  • You will participate by entering responses on a computer in the lab of the Experimental Economics Center.
Future Research

We will not retain any information that could personally identify you. We may use your anonymous responses in the experiment for future research. We will not ask for consent from you if we do this.

Risks

You will not have any more risks than you would in a normal day of life.

Benefits

This study is not designed to benefit you personally. We hope to gain information about how individuals make decisions in common-pool resource environments and to assess the impact of partial output-sharing arrangements on resource use.

Alternatives

The alternative to taking part in this study is to not take part in the study.

Compensation

If you decide to participate, you will receive compensation in U.S. dollars at the end of the experiment session. The amount of compensation will be determined by your decisions and the decisions of anonymous members in your group.

Voluntary Participation and Withdrawal

You do not have to be in this study. If you decide to be in the study and change your mind, you can drop out at any time.

Confidentiality

We will keep your records private to the extent required by law. The following people and groups will have access to the information you provide:

  • The study PI and Co-PI and members of their research team.
  • GSU Institutional Review Board
  • Office for Human Research Protection (OHRP)
  • GSU Research Services and Administration

We will use a study participant number rather than your name or other personally-identifying information in study records.

We will store the information you provide on password and firewall-protected cloud servers.

When we present or publish the results of this study, we will not use information that may identify you.

Contact Information

  • If you have questions, concerns, or complaints about the study

You can contact the IRB for questions, concerns, problems, information, input, or questions about your rights as a research participant. Contact the IRB at 404-413-3500 or irb@gsu.edu.
